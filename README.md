# ternary-knot

**Tying knots in three-valued string. Reidemeister moves, braid groups, and Z₃ invariants.**

A knot is a closed loop embedded in 3D space. Two knots are equivalent if one can be deformed into the other without cutting — captured by the three Reidemeister moves. The central question of knot theory: when are two knots the same?

In ternary knot theory, every crossing is labeled {-1, 0, +1}: under-crossing, virtual crossing, or over-crossing. This gives us a natural Z₃-valued knot invariant — the sum of crossing signs weighted by structure, modulo 3. The ternary Jones polynomial (computed via a simplified Kauffman bracket) gives another Z₃ invariant.

This crate implements: knot diagrams with ternary crossings, Reidemeister moves (type I and II), writhe and linking number computation, braid words with generators σᵢ and σᵢ⁻¹, braid closure (Alexander's theorem), alternating knot detection, and a ternary Jones polynomial.

## What's Inside

- **`Crossing`** — over (+1), under (-1), or virtual (0) crossing between two strands
- **`KnotDiagram`** — a knot as a sequence of crossings
- **`reidemeister_1_remove/add`** — type I Reidemeister moves (twist/untwist)
- **`reidemeister_2_remove/add`** — type II Reidemeister moves (cancel/add crossing pairs)
- **`writhe(diagram)`** — sum of crossing signs (invariant under R2 and R3)
- **`linking_number(diagram, a, b)`** — how many times two components link
- **`is_alternating(diagram)`** — do over/under crossings alternate?
- **`ternary_knot_invariant(diagram)`** — Z₃-valued invariant
- **`BraidWord`** — a braid as a sequence of generators σᵢ
- **`jones_polynomial_ternary(diagram)`** — Kauffman bracket mod 3

## Quick Example

```rust
use ternary_knot::*;

// Right-handed trefoil: three over-crossings
let trefoil = KnotDiagram::new(vec![
    Crossing::over(0, 1),
    Crossing::over(0, 1),
    Crossing::over(0, 1),
], 1);

assert_eq!(writhe(&trefoil), 3);
assert_eq!(writhe_mod3(&trefoil), 0);

// Simplify via Reidemeister II
let mut k = KnotDiagram::new(vec![
    Crossing::over(0, 1),
    Crossing::under(0, 1),
], 1);
assert!(k.reidemeister_2_remove()); // cancelled!
assert_eq!(k.crossings.len(), 0);
```

## The Deeper Truth

**Knots in ternary space are combinatorial objects, not topological ones.** In real knot theory, you need continuous deformations and the full power of algebraic topology. In ternary, everything is discrete: a knot is a sequence of labeled crossings, and equivalence is generated by three local rewrite rules (Reidemeister moves).

The Z₃ knot invariant reveals something beautiful: the trefoil knot has writhe 3, which is 0 modulo 3. The figure-eight knot has writhe 0. Both are "invisible" to the Z₃ writhe invariant — you need the full Jones polynomial (or Kauffman bracket) to distinguish them. But the Jones polynomial, evaluated at the cube root of unity, collapses to a single Z₃ value. This is the deepest layer of ternary knot theory: the polynomial invariants, when restricted to Z₃, become elementary.
